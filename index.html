By simplifying this navigation (and making the nav bar consistent throughout) a user can now navigate the entire application from any screen. My Process

Every project is different, so the process usually evolves and changes to fit a project's needs. I like to use the Design Thinking process to help understand the user, define the challenges they face, and create innovative solutions to prototype and test.

Research

  • Begin by immersing myself in the user's perspective. Through interviews, polls, observations, and market research. I like to develop a deep understanding of the users needs, challenges, and emotions to inform the design process.

Definition

  • Using my research I can translate those insights into a clear problem statement. Defining the challenge at hand and setting the path for the design process.

Ideate

  • This is an area of my greates strengths. Having brainstorming sessions with my team or just me individually. I explore a variety of ideas without judgment to foster creativity. My goal is to generate a diverse range of potential solutions to address the defined problem. I am fairly quuick with ideating solutions but I always like to make sure I am getting it right. Most of the time I find that I get it right by finding bad or wrong solutions. Keeping a good list of my top UX laws has always helped me in finding solutions.

Prototyping

  • Translate the ideas into tangible representations. I like to start by creating low-fidelity prototypes that allow for quick testing and iteration. Having a good component librrary can make this work much faster and keep visual consistency in place.

Testing

  • I can use my prototypes to gain valuable feedback early in the design process. This can happen with more user testing, team feedback, stakeholder feedback, and a wide variety of user type feedback.

Dev Hand Off

  • Getting technical feedback and having the flexibility to make changes has always been key to a successful implementation for me. The prototyping cylce doesnt stop after that step in the process. Once I have gotten all the feedback I need I can make my low-fi mock ups into more pixel perfect mocks for our devs. In this phase I make sure everything is in alignment with our style guide. I use figma for my mock ups and add as many developer notes that I can to make sure hand off is smooth. There will always be questions, concerns, or even some microinteractions that are missed. But keeping a good open communication with our devs is always a strenght of mine.

Iteration

  • Once dev handoff is done and they have implemented the product feature I like to continue testing, polling, and watching for live data. I have been using Pendo to track user paths, funnels, and even feature playbacks to make sure we havent missed anything and that our research holds up to a live environment. On bigger features I like to implement an alpha and beta roll out. Getting that user feedback is always extremely valuable. Working with an agile methodology, I always like to be open to iterations that can improve the usr experience.
